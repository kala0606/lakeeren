Draft bio — please edit Ujjwal Agarwal is an artist and creative technologist working at the meeting point of generative systems, real-time camera and sound work, and drawing machines. His practice treats software as a material — writing the rules of a system and then drawing, playing, and performing from it, across screens, paper, and the live body.

01

KARMA

Year 2023Medium Generative algorithmReleased Art Blocks

“Karma, the original algorithm of Indian thought. Action, inaction, randomness, will — all are ground in its maw to determine the manifest shape of the next moment. The past makes the present, and shall do so in the future. But what one sees is the eternal present, and from there, the past is only the fading imprint on memory — an imprint that leaves us with the perception of pattern, as gratifying as it is mystical. The past’s fading, thus, is a necessity.”

12

hawa.live

Year 2026Medium Voice journal, webMode Live website

You talk. HAWA listens. It records your voice and turns it into a private, sealed journal — your words transcribed, compressed into a small insight by an AI called WIND, then stamped with time, place and a cryptographic hash that chains each entry to every one before it. Thoughts become a tamper-proof record; a live stream and a world summary read the collective mood. The shader below is the site's living background — audio-reactive particle terrain.

hawa.live

Hosted and running as its own site — open it, hold the record button, and whisper.
